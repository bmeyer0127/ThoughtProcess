I figured I could share my thought process of doing something that has been quite typical in my day-to-day life recently.
Specifically, that is how I like to build a website.
And it's only fitting that I build a website to show how I build a website.

Content

content before code

solid concept of information
illustrated draft of layout
idea of necessary tools

During this phase I am, more or less, creating a foundation in my head and on paper. Ideas may change and words will adapt, of course, but this process creates the static concept of what will be built.

Code

code before cascade...
sometimes

layout before style
font and general position may be my personal exception
finished layout helps visualize ideal style

Here is where the visual structure begins to manifest. It is where I begin to lay the foundation for the later phases. Styling is (almost) entirely ommited during this.

Cascade

cascade before coming alive

more precise positioning
appropriate coloring and styling
intended aesthetic implemented

This area and the next are focused primarily on aesthetic and the visual human experience. It is much more about fine tuning and polishing. This is where I begin to let loose a little and try out weird and interesting designs.

Coming Alive

adding interactivity

javascript
functionality
animations

Much like the cascade section, this area is very much about the human experience. But rather than being purely visual, this is more focused on interactivity and maneuverability.
